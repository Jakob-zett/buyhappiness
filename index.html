Step 1: The idea

Every year on black friday the profit of retailers around the world peak. Their numbers are “in the black”. And like that black friday, over the years, became a day about the money. Good deals for consumers and high profits for retailers.
In 2014 this meant $3,3 billion spent at the 100 biggest retailers alone. We spent an average of $290.
At its core it is a day on which we give money to those who already have enough and in return mostly get stuff we do not really need. And a good deal may get us excited for a day or two but it does not make us truly happy.
But we can turn this idea around once more. We can turn a day about profit into a day about happiness.
We can turn black friday into happy friday.

Step 2: The concept

That is why we assembled a few non-profits for you right here. And you can select your cause and help those in need, creating lasting happiness for both parties.

Step 3:The (larger) concept

This website is only a small operation. However, there are plenty of big retailers out there reaching millions of people on black friday. We could to get them to implement a happyfriday-category, a category for the good cause, on their website and start celebrating happy friday around the world.
